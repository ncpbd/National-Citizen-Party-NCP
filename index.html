Formation of the National Citizen Party (NCP)

In 2024, disillusioned political leaders formed the National Citizen Party (NCP) to address corruption and advocate for citizen-driven change.

  1. Jun
    June 2024

    Initiation of Quota Reform Movement

    The movement for public sector job quota reform sparked across Bangladesh, with students demanding equal opportunities and an end to discriminatory practices in the hiring process.

  2. Jul
    July 2024

    Escalation to Mass Uprising

    As protests grew, clashes between law enforcement and demonstrators intensified. The violent crackdown by the government resulted in multiple deaths, escalating the situation into a nationwide uprising.

  3. Aug
    August 5, 2024

    Ouster of Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina

    The government’s response to the protests culminated in the resignation of Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina. She was forced into exile following the massive uprising, marking a major political shift.

    In the aftermath of Hasina’s removal, the Jatiya Nagorik Committee (National Citizens' Committee) was formed to advocate for a more inclusive and democratic political landscape.

About National Citizens Party (NCP)

The Jatiya Nagorik Party (National Citizen's Party or NCP) is a political party in Bangladesh. It was established on February 28, 2025, through the initiative of student rights activists and the Jatiya Nagorik Committee (National Citizen's Committee). It is notable for being the first student-led political party in Bangladesh's history.

Context of Establishment:

  • The party was formed under the leadership of young leaders from the July uprising.
  • It was created through the efforts of student rights advocates and the National Citizen's Committee.
  • It was established to fight for the rights of the student community.
